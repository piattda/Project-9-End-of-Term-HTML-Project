Accreditation

How do we show that we are qualified to serve as an interpreter? Who legitemizes us; codefies our roles; assigns us our role in the court room?

Legitemacy comes from several sources:

  • State Supreme Court Certification - All states have legislation in place to allow for interpreters. These governing bodies also provide a certification exam that can be taken to become officially acknowledged as a qualified interpreter in that state. This type of credential is very valuable, a state government formally legitemizes the inerpreter.
  • Degrees - A bachelor degree, masters degree, or phd also serve to legitemize an interpreter.
  • Certificates - Several universities offer translation certificates.
  • Masters in conference interpreting - some universities offer a 1-2 yr masters degree. If you wish to work in the EU Parliment this will be useful to know.

American Translators Association

1hr ago

The American Translators Association (ATA) is a professional organization that offers translation certifications, professional development resources like conferences and seminars, and helps interpreters and translators advertise their services.

  • If you plan to work as a freelance interpreter or translator paying dues to the ATA is a great way to find consistent, well paying work.
  • If you need to find an interpreter or translator you can use the ATA's directory.
